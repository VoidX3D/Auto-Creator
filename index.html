1. Objective

To measure the dimensions of one cylindrical object and one spherical object and calculate their Curved Surface Area, Total Surface Area, and Volume using π = 22/7.

2. Materials Used

  • One cylindrical container
  • One spherical ball
  • Ruler
  • Calculator
  • Notebook and pen

3. Procedure

  1. Select one cylinder and one sphere for measurement.
  2. Measure diameter using a ruler.
  3. Calculate radius by dividing diameter by two.
  4. For cylinder, measure height.
  5. Substitute the values in CSA, TSA, and Volume formulas using π = 22/7.
  6. Record step-by-step calculations clearly.

4. Measurements

Cylinder: Diameter = 7 cm, Radius = 3.5 cm, Height = 22 cm

Sphere: Diameter = 6 cm, Radius = 3 cm

5. Calculations

A. Cylinder

CSA: 2πrh = 2 × 22/7 × 3.5 × 22 = 484 cm²

TSA: 2πr(r+h) = 2 × 22/7 × 3.5 × 25.5 = 561 cm²

Volume: πr²h = 22/7 × (3.5)² × 22 = 847 cm³

B. Sphere

TSA: 4πr² = 4 × 22/7 × 3² = 113.14 cm²

Volume: 4/3πr³ = 4/3 × 22/7 × 27 = 113.14 cm³
